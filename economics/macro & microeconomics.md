## Information Economics

- [The Market for “Lemons”: Quality Uncertainty and the Market Mechanism](https://www.jstor.org/stable/1879431) - Akerlof’s foundational paper on asymmetric information, adverse selection and the possibility that low-quality products can drive high-quality products out of a market.

## Behavioral Economics and Rational Choice

- [A Theory of Rational Addiction](https://doi.org/10.1086/261558) - Becker and Murphy’s influential model treating addictive consumption as part of a forward-looking and internally consistent utility-maximizing plan.

- [The Theory of Rational Addiction](https://doi.org/10.1111/add.14822) - Ole Rogeberg’s critical review of the rational-addiction framework and its limitations as an explanation of real-world addictive behavior.

## Quantum Economics

- [Quantum Propensity in Economics](https://doi.org/10.3389/frai.2021.772294) - Proposes a probabilistic framework for economic decision-making inspired by quantum probability, quantum computing and propensity functions.
